The historic chapel of Monte Grande is a historic place that comes from the beginings of the city. It's located at the main plaza of the city. Believers of all over the city to the chapel for special religious events.

Mitre Plaza is the main plaza in the city, being a main point of reunion for many people. You will find the townhall, the main chapel, schools, police departments, banks and more in it's sorroundings.

Monte Grande's historic watertank was one of the main public water tanks in the city long time ago. Now it's renewed and painted and is one of the most recognized historic places in the city. Citizens may also call it a 'UFO' due its particular shape.

Alem Street is the main street of the city, where most of the restaurants and stores are located. Some weekends of the year this street is closed for transit and people can walk on the streets not worring about cars.

Train is one of the most used public transport in Buenos Aires, Argentina. Monte Grande has its own station, taking a train in Monte Grande can take you to downtown in about 30 minutes for a super low cost.
